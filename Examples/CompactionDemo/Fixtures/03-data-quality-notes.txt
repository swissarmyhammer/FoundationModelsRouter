Data-quality notes for Nightjar: roughly four percent of entries are
missing a wind-direction reading. A missing reading is always recorded as
a bare dash rather than an omitted field, so parsers must treat a lone
dash as an explicit missing value rather than a parse failure — treating
it as a failure would reject otherwise-valid rows and silently shrink the
archive.

A smaller number of entries (well under one percent) carry a temperature
reading with an obviously transposed decimal point, identified by being
more than five standard deviations from that outpost's seasonal mean;
these are flagged for manual review rather than auto-corrected, since
auto-correcting a transposed decimal can silently produce a wrong value
that looks plausible. Reply with one short sentence acknowledging you have
read this.
